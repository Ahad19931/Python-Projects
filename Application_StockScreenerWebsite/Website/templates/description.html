Candle Stick Chart Description

  • A candlestick chart (also called Japanese candlestick chart) is a style of financial chart used to describe price movements of a security, derivative, or currency.
  • Candlestick charts are a visual aid for decision making in stock, foreign exchange, commodity, and option trading.
  • Looking at a candlestick, one can identify an assetâ€™s opening and closing prices, highs and lows, and overall range for a specific time frame.
  • The area between the open and the close is called the real body, price excursions above and below the real body are shadows (also called wicks).
  • Wicks illustrate the highest and lowest traded prices of an asset during the time interval represented. The body illustrates the opening and closing trades.
  • If the asset closed higher than it opened, the body is hollow or filled with a particular color(green), with the opening price at the bottom of the body and the closing price at the top.
  • If the asset closed lower than it opened, the body is solid or filled with a particular color(red), with the opening price at the top of the body and the closing price at the bottom.
